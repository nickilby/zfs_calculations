Understanding URE (Unrecoverable Read Error) Risk

What is a URE?

A Unrecoverable Read Error (URE) occurs when a drive cannot return a requested block of data correctly. Drives have advertised URE rates:

  • Consumer SATA drives: ~1 in 10¹⁴ bits read
  • Enterprise SATA/SAS drives: ~1 in 10¹⁵ bits read
  • Enterprise NVME drives: ~1 in 10¹⁶ bits read

Practical Impact

In human terms, this translates to:

  • Consumer drives (1e14): 1 error per 12.5 TB read
  • Enterprise drives (1e15): 1 error per 125 TB read
  • Enterprise NVME drives (1e16): 1 error per 1250 TB read

Every 12.5 TB read with consumer drives has a ~37% chance of at least one URE.

RAIDZ1 Resilver Risk

During a RAIDZ1 resilver (e.g., 6×8TB = 48TB raw), you must read ~40TB from surviving drives:

  • Consumer disks: ~96% chance of URE during resilver
  • Enterprise disks: ~28% chance of URE during resilver
  • Enterprise NVME disks: ~3% chance of URE during resilver

Critical: A single URE during RAIDZ1 resilver = pool failure, since you've already lost one drive and cannot recover that bad sector.
